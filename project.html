Reasoning and project point

Why Burnout Beacon matters

The project point is simple: students should not have to wait until they are in crisis to notice that their routines are shifting in unhealthy ways.

Pitch Hook

Imagine if your car only warned you after the engine failed.

The warning should come earlier.

Student well-being often works like a dashboard with no warning lights. Most students do not realize they are struggling until exhaustion or declining performance has already taken hold. Burnout Beacon brings predictive maintenance to well-being by showing early signals clearly.

Hackathon Alignment

Four reasons the project fits the challenge.

Make the Invisible Visible

Highlights gradual stress, sleep, workload, and motivation shifts before they become obvious.

Check Engine Light

Warns students about unusual patterns without claiming to diagnose mental health conditions.

Predictive Maintenance

Applies the same early-warning logic used for machines to student routines and recovery.

Social Support

Turns communities into useful support systems through study groups, clubs, and peer options.

How the Prototype Thinks

An explainable rules engine, not a black box.

The interactive demo uses weighted signals to create a pressure score. The score is translated into a status, and every status comes with plain-language reasons and next steps.

1

Inputs

Sleep, consistency, deadlines, stress, energy, motivation, connectedness, and recovery time.

2

Rules

Higher risk comes from poor sleep, heavy workload, high stress, low energy, and isolation.

3

Outputs

A wellness status, a pressure score, explanations, recommendations, and community support.
